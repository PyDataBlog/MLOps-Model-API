\section*{4.4 Exercise 1}
\subsection*{a}
Suppose there exists a feasible solution $x$ to the program (P) 
$$
\begin{matrix}
\max & 0 \cdot x \\
\text{subject to}& \\
& Ax \leq b \\
& x \geq 0
\end{matrix}
$$
and a feasible solution $y$ to the dual problem (D)
$$
\begin{matrix}
\min & b \cdot y \\
\text{subject to}& \\
& A^Ty \geq 0 \\
& y \geq 0
\end{matrix}
$$ 
By strong duality, $\exists \bar{x}, \bar{y}$ s.t. $\bar{x} \cdot c = \bar{y} \cdot b$. This implies that $\bar{y} \cdot b  = 0$ since $c = 0$. Since $\bar{y}$ is optimal, for all feasible $y$ in (D), $y \cdot b \geq \bar{y} \cdot b$. So, $\nexists y$ with $ y\cdot b < 0$. 
Now, suppose there exists a $y$ with $  b \cdot y < 0$. Then let  $ y_\lambda = \lambda y$ where $ \lambda > 0 $. $y_\lambda$ is feasible and  $  y_\lambda \cdot b =  \lambda y \cdot b <0$. Then, for $\lambda > 1$ $ y_\lambda \cdot b < y \cdot b$.  Therefore, (D) is unbounded and  Corollary 4.2  implies (P) is infeasible.

\subsection*{b}
Suppose there exists a feasible $x$ to the program (P) 
$$
\begin{matrix}
\max & 1 \cdot x \\
\text{subject to}& \\
& Ax = 0 \\
& x \geq 0
\end{matrix}
$$
With dual (D)
$$
\begin{matrix}
\min & 0 \cdot y \\
\text{subject to}& \\
& A^Ty \geq 1 
\end{matrix}
$$
Suppose $x \neq 0$.Suppose $ \exists y$ with $A^T y = d > 0$. Then let $ y' = y / \min d$. Then, $ A^T y ' \geq 1 $, so $y'$ is feasible. Thus, by weak duality, $x \cdot 1 \leq y' \cdot 0 = 0$, but $x \cdot 1 >0$, which is a contradiction.\\
If $\exists$ $y>0$, then we can define $y'$ as before which forces $x = 0$ to ensure weak duality is satisfied.
