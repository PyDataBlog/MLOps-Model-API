\subsection{Detailed Description}
Four-\/bin P\-D\-F wave climate object following Ashton \& Murray (2006). 

\hyperlink{classWave}{Wave} directions represented in a four-\/bin P\-D\-F of wave directions. \hyperlink{classWave}{Wave} climate thus described by two parameters\-: asymmetry (A) and highness (U). Assymetry is the proportion of waves approaching from the upcoast direction (relative to the overall trend of the coastline). Highness is the proportion of waves that impinge the coastline at high angle ($>$45$^\wedge$o). See Ashton, A.\-D., Murray, A.\-B., 2006. High-\/angle wave instability and emergent shoreline shapes\-: 1. Modeling of sand waves, flying spits, and capes. J. Geophys. Res., 111(F4), F04011, doi\-:\href{https://dx.doi.org/10.1029/2005jf000422}{\tt 10.\-1029/2005jf000422.}. \hyperlink{classWave}{Wave} height and period represented by Gaussian distributions. \begin{DoxyAuthor}{Author}
