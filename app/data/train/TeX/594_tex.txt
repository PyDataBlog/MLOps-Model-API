{\tmstrong{Cars and goats: the Monty Hall dilemma}}

On Sunday September 9, 1990, the following question appeared in the ``Ask
Marilyn'' column in Parade, a Sunday supplement to many newspapers across the
United States:
\begin{itemizedot}
  \item Suppose you're on a game show, and you're given the choice of three
  doors;
  
  \item behind one door is a car; behind the others, goats.
  
  \item You pick a door, say No. 1, and the host, who knows what's behind the
  doors, opens another door, say No. 3, which has a goat.
  
  \item He then says to you, ``Do you want to pick door No. 2?''
  
  \item Is it to your advantage to switch your choice?---Craig F. Whitaker,
  Columbia, Md.
\end{itemizedot}
\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \
\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \
\href{https://www.boards.ie/b/thread/2056147144}{https://www.boards.ie/b/thread/2056147144}

Inital guess, Suppose picking No.1 door:
\begin{eqnarray*}
  P ( \tmop{car}_{1} | \tmop{pick}_{1} ) & = & \frac{1}{3}\\
  P ( \tmop{car}_{2} | \tmop{pick}_{1} ) & = & \frac{1}{3}\\
  P ( \tmop{car}_{3} | \tmop{pick}_{1} ) & = & \frac{1}{3}
\end{eqnarray*}
Open a door with a goat:
\begin{eqnarray*}
  P ( \tmop{open}_{2} | \tmop{car}_{1} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) & = & \frac{1}{2}\\
  P ( \tmop{open}_{3} | \tmop{car}_{1} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) & = & \frac{1}{2}\\
  P ( \tmop{open}_{2} | \tmop{car}_{2} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) & = & 0\\
  P ( \tmop{open}_{3} | \tmop{car}_{2} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) & = & 1\\
  P ( \tmop{open}_{2} | \tmop{car}_{3} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) & = & 1\\
  P ( \tmop{open}_{3} | \tmop{car}_{3} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) & = & 0
\end{eqnarray*}
Posterior probability:
\begin{eqnarray*}
  P ( \tmop{car}_{1} | \tmop{pick}_{1} , \tmop{open}_{2} ) & = & \frac{P (
  \tmop{open}_{2} | \tmop{car}_{1} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{1} |
  \tmop{pick}_{1} )}{\sum_{i=1}^{3} P ( \tmop{open}_{2} | \tmop{car}_{i} ,
  \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{i} | \tmop{pick}_{1} )}\\
  & = & \frac{\frac{1}{2} \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}{\frac{1}{2} \cdummy
  \frac{1}{3} +0 \cdummy \frac{1}{3} +1 \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}\\
  & = & \frac{1}{3}\\
  P ( \tmop{car}_{1} | \tmop{pick}_{1} , \tmop{open}_{3} ) & = & \frac{P (
  \tmop{open}_{3} | \tmop{car}_{1} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{1} |
  \tmop{pick}_{1} )}{\sum_{i=1}^{3} P ( \tmop{open}_{3} | \tmop{car}_{i} ,
  \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{i} | \tmop{pick}_{1} )}\\
  & = & \frac{\frac{1}{2} \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}{\frac{1}{2} \cdummy
  \frac{1}{3} +1 \cdummy \frac{1}{3} +0 \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}\\
  & = & \frac{1}{3}\\
  P ( \tmop{car}_{2} | \tmop{pick}_{1} , \tmop{open}_{2} ) & = & \frac{P (
  \tmop{open}_{2} | \tmop{car}_{2} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{2} |
  \tmop{pick}_{1} )}{\sum_{i=1}^{3} P ( \tmop{open}_{2} | \tmop{car}_{i} ,
  \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{i} | \tmop{pick}_{1} )}\\
  & = & \frac{0 \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}{\frac{1}{2} \cdummy \frac{1}{3} +0
  \cdummy \frac{1}{3} +1 \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}\\
  & = & 0\\
  P ( \tmop{car}_{3} | \tmop{pick}_{1} , \tmop{open}_{2} ) & = & \frac{P (
  \tmop{open}_{2} | \tmop{car}_{3} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{3} |
  \tmop{pick}_{1} )}{\sum_{i=1}^{3} P ( \tmop{open}_{2} | \tmop{car}_{i} ,
  \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{i} | \tmop{pick}_{1} )}\\
  & = & \frac{1 \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}{\frac{1}{2} \cdummy \frac{1}{3} +0
  \cdummy \frac{1}{3} +1 \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}\\
  & = & \frac{2}{3}\\
  P ( \tmop{car}_{3} | \tmop{pick}_{1} , \tmop{open}_{3} ) & = & \frac{P (
  \tmop{open}_{3} | \tmop{car}_{3} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{3} |
  \tmop{pick}_{1} )}{\sum_{i=1}^{3} P ( \tmop{open}_{3} | \tmop{car}_{i} ,
  \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{i} | \tmop{pick}_{1} )}\\
  & = & \frac{0 \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}{\frac{1}{2} \cdummy \frac{1}{3} +1
  \cdummy \frac{1}{3} +0 \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}\\
  & = & 0\\
  P ( \tmop{car}_{2} | \tmop{pick}_{1} , \tmop{open}_{3} ) & = & \frac{P (
  \tmop{open}_{3} | \tmop{car}_{2} , \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{2} |
  \tmop{pick}_{1} )}{\sum_{i=1}^{3} P ( \tmop{open}_{3} | \tmop{car}_{i} ,
  \tmop{pick}_{1} ) P ( \tmop{car}_{i} | \tmop{pick}_{1} )}\\
  & = & \frac{1 \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}{\frac{1}{2} \cdummy \frac{1}{3} +1
  \cdummy \frac{1}{3} +0 \cdummy \frac{1}{3}}\\
  & = & \frac{2}{3}
\end{eqnarray*}
Then it shows that:

\ \ \ Switch to another closed door can increase the probability of winning
from $\frac{1}{3}$ to $\frac{2}{3}$ .
