Solving the equation
\[
\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial \phi} = \left(\hat{\bf{e}}_\theta + \hat{\bf{e}}_\phi\right)\cdot\nabla f
\]
on a 2D $\left(\theta, \phi\right)$ doubly periodic domain with sides of length $2\pi$.

Transform into coordinates
\begin{eqnarray*}
y &=& \theta \\
z &=& \phi - \int_{\theta_0}^\theta \nu d\theta
\end{eqnarray*}
with pitch $\nu$. Taking the gradient of these expressions, and
using $\nabla = \nabla y \frac{\partial}{\partial y} + \nabla z\frac{\partial}{\partial z}$ assuming
\[
\left|\nabla\theta\right| = 1 \qquad \left|\nabla\phi\right| = 1
\]
gives
\begin{eqnarray*}
\nabla y &=& \nabla \theta \\
\nabla z &=& \nabla \phi - \nu \nabla \theta
\end{eqnarray*}

\[
\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} = \left[ \left(1 + \nu\right) \nabla y + \nabla z \right] \cdot \nabla f
\]
which when expanded gives
\begin{eqnarray*}
\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} &=& \underbrace{\left(1 + \nu\right)}_{v_y}\left[g^{yy} \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} + g^{yz}\frac{\partial f}{\partial z}\right] \\
&+& \underbrace{1}_{v_z}\left[g^{zz} \frac{\partial f}{\partial z} + g^{yz}\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\right]
\end{eqnarray*}
with
\[
g^{yy} = 1 \qquad g^{yz} = -\nu \qquad g^{zz} = 1 + \nu^2
\]
