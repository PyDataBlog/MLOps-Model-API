<center><H3>New Corresponding Fellows at NORDITA</H3></center>
NORDITA warmly welcomes <b>Johannes (Hans) Bijnens</b> and
<b>Tomas Bohr</b> as new corresponding fellows.
<p>After his Ph. D. studies at Caltech on chiral perturbation
theory and the weak interaction in 1985, 
<b>Hans Bijnens </b>was a postdoc at DESY 1985-1987, 
an assistant at Munich University 1987-1989, and a CERN
fellow 1990-92. In the following years he held a position as assistant
professor at Nordita and since March 1997 he has been at the University
of Lund, Department of Theoretical Physics 2 as a Forskartj&auml;nst.
<br>His main present areas of research are:
<br>
i) Chiral Perturbation Theory: a low-energy effective
theory of the strong interactions and one of the few model independent
tools available in hadronic physics.
<br>
ii) CP-violation and standard model parameters: quark
masses, mixing angles and the CP-violating mechanisms in and beyond 
the
standard model. This involves general studies of the kaon and 
B-meson system.
<br>
iii) Strong interaction effects in low-energy precision
experiments used for the study of the weak interaction and 
physics beyond
the standard model physics.
<br>
iv) Connections between chiral perturbation theory, perturbative
QCD and various phenomenological strong interaction models.
<p>
<b>Tomas Bohr</b>'s interests will be presented in the next
number of NORDITA News.

<b>NORDITA MEETING ON 
LOW-DIMENSIONAL PHYSICS IN SOLIDS AND TRAPPED ATOMS</b>
<br>August 31 - September 2, 2000.
<P>
The aim of this meeting is to gather physicists working in
the general area of low-dimensional physics and atomic Bose condensates,
with talks on various subjects, like the quantum Hall effect, 
high Tc superconductivity, Bose condensation and integrable models. 
During the last couple of years there has been quite some interest 
in two-dimensional aspects of Bose condensation,
and there turn out to be close similarities between the physics 
of these systems and the quantum Hall effect and quantum dots. 
We thus believe that it will be very fruitful to bring these two 
communities together.
<p>Further information and registration forms can be found at 
<a
href="http://www.nordita.dk/~susanne/conf/">http://www.nordita.dk/~susanne/conf/</a>.</b>
